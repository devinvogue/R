CLIMATE CHANGE DEBATES

“Young people have the highest stake in the success of the Paris Climate Change Agreement” – Angelica Shamerina, UNDP-GEF

Climate change is one of the major challenges of our time and adds considerable stress to our societies and to the environment. From shifting weather patterns that threaten food production, to rising sea levels that increase the risk of catastrophic flooding, the impacts of climate change are global in scope and unprecedented in scale. Without drastic action today, adapting to these impacts in the future will be more difficult and costly. our planet enters a period of changing climate never before experienced in recorded human history, interest is growing in the potential for deliberate intervention in the climate system to counter climate change. According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change(IPCC), only immediate and sustained action will stop climate change from causing irreversible and potentially catastrophic damage to our environment.

Education, participatory advocacy and awareness-raising play an essential role in increasing the climate change adaptation and mitigation capacities of communities by building the adaptive capacity of communities as well as ensuring that individuals make informed decisions. Education helps learners understand the causes and consequences of climate change, prepares them to live with the impacts of climate change, and empowers women and men to adopt more sustainable lifestyles.

As shown by Climate Change and Environmental Education, UNICEF, Climate change directly affects the Sustainable development goals (SGDs). Education is an essential element of the global response to climate change. It helps young people understand and address the impact of global warming, encourages changes in their attitudes and behavior and helps them adapt to climate change-related trends.

The program seeks to engage second cycle institutions in a very participatory interactive learning process by engaging the students in interactive discussions in the requisite information to make informed decisions and skills on how they can impact their community positively and inspire others to act.

A truly inspiring effort by students who would devout time to practicing to succeed in this intellectually exciting activity, Climate change ambassadors and movement will be formed in secondary schools, the participating student ambassadors will be trained with support from Environmental Protection Agency to embark on mitigation activities such as Tree planting and community education programs. Media materials would be produced for social media engagement and education.

The four participating schools would be participating in an interschool debate for the climate Change awareness program which would be televised on national television.

The theme for this year’s climate debate is ‘Empowering the younger generation through advocacy and pragmatic interventions using local innovations to enhance disaster risk reduction and climate adaptation resilience in Africa’


OBJECTIVES

  • Create climate change awareness amongst the younger generation and equipping them with the requisite knowledge to make decisions on the mitigation and adaptation on climate change.
  • Create a national movement on climate conscious youth in Ghana.
  • Use art, multimedia, social media to drive and engage young people in fighting for and demanding positive climate actions from their local authorities and immediate environments.
  • Spark a behavioral change which would help with adaptation to climate change.
